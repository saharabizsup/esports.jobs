# Style & Brand Direction

## Brand Attributes
- Competitive yet inclusive
- Professional with a touch of playful energy
- Data-informed and trustworthy

## Visual Inspiration
- Esports broadcast overlays with bold typography and neon accents
- Modern recruitment platforms emphasizing clarity and hierarchy
- Minimalist dashboards with dark mode options

## Color Palette (Draft)
- **Primary:** Electric indigo (#5A4BFF) for calls-to-action
- **Secondary:** Neon turquoise (#35F2C0) for highlights and accents
- **Neutral:** Charcoal gray (#1E1E24), Slate gray (#3A3A42), Cloud white (#F5F7FA)
- **Alert:** Victory red (#FF4F64) for critical states

## Typography
- **Display:** "Bebas Neue" or "Teko" for headers to reflect esports energy
- **Body:** "Inter" or "Satoshi" for readability in long-form job descriptions
- **Monospace/Code:** "JetBrains Mono" for technical snippets in portfolios

## Imagery & Iconography
- Showcase real players, coaches, and analysts in action or preparation
- Incorporate geometric patterns inspired by arena LED panels
- Use line-based icons with slight neon glows for continuity

## Voice & Tone
- Confident, encouraging, and community-focused
- Avoid jargon unless contextual to esports roles
- Celebrate achievements and progression stories

## Layout Principles
- Emphasize scannability for job seekers and hiring managers
- Use card-based modules with clear hierarchy and status indicators
- Support both light and dark themes without sacrificing accessibility

## Accessibility Checklist
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text elements
- Provide keyboard navigable interactions
- Offer alternative text for imagery and multimedia portfolios

## Next Steps
- Develop component library tokens aligned with palette and typography
- Create hero, list, and profile page mockups for user testing
- Validate color contrast and typography pairings with target audience
